
Here’s a tight, leadership-friendly talk track you can use while presenting this slide. It’s structured as a story with clear progression and business impact.

⸻

Opening (10–15 sec)

“Let me walk you through how our AI SDLC journey has evolved—from isolated prompting to a scalable, reusable engineering practice.”

⸻

Step 1 – Started with Copilot & Cursor (Problem Framing)

“Initially, we started leveraging tools like Copilot and Cursor to accelerate development.
It gave us a quick boost in productivity, but we quickly hit a limitation—our prompts were disposable.

Every time we generated code, the context lived only in that moment.
If we had to revisit or modify something, we were essentially starting over—rebuilding context, rethinking prompts, and losing time.

So while speed improved, consistency and reusability were still gaps.”

👉 Pause briefly → sets up problem clearly

⸻

Step 2 – Saved Prompts in GitHub (Intermediate Improvement)

“To address that, we moved towards saving prompts in GitHub and sharing them across teams.

This was a big step forward—it introduced collaboration and some level of reuse.
Teams didn’t have to reinvent everything from scratch anymore.

But prompts alone still had limitations:

* They weren’t structured
* Hard to scale across use cases
* Still required manual interpretation and context rebuilding”

👉 Position this as progress, but not sufficient

⸻

Step 3 – Adopted SDD (Transformation)

“That’s where we evolved into Spec-Driven Development—SDD.

Instead of treating prompts as one-off inputs, we started treating them as structured, reusable specifications that both humans and AI agents can understand.

This fundamentally changed how we build:

* Context is preserved
* Outputs are consistent
* Development becomes repeatable and scalable”

⸻

Outcome / Business Impact (Very Important for Leadership)

“The impact is clear:

* Higher quality output with less rework
* Faster delivery because context is not rebuilt every time
* Consistency across teams and projects

We’ve effectively moved from ad-hoc AI usage to a disciplined AI-driven SDLC.”

⸻

Closing (Strong Finish)

“This shift is what enables us to scale AI adoption across teams—not just as a tool, but as a standardized way of building software.”

⸻

Optional Punch Line (if you want impact)

“We didn’t just improve developer productivity—we made AI work reusable.”
